Welcome to the World of Exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside of our own solar system. Using powerful telescopes and space missions like Kepler and TESS, we've been able to discover thousands of these distant worlds. Some of them even have the potential to be Earth-like while others are completely alien.

Exoplanets can have multiple terrains including rocky, gaseous, or icy. Some are relatively the same size as Earth while others can be compared to gas giants larger than Jupiter.

Using methods like the transit technique and radial velocity, astronomers detect exoplanets by observing their star's light or motion.
